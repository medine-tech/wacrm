# Delegation and Empowerment

## Overall Summary

This lesson explores how effective delegation goes beyond task assignment to become a tool for team empowerment. It covers the delegation spectrum from directive to autonomous, common fears managers face when delegating, and practical frameworks for matching delegation level to team member readiness.

## Key Points

- Delegation is not abdication — it requires clear expectations and follow-up
- The Situational Leadership model maps delegation level to competence and commitment
- Fear of losing control is the #1 barrier to effective delegation
- Empowerment requires psychological safety — team members must feel safe to make mistakes
- The RACI matrix clarifies who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed

## Detailed Notes

### The Delegation Spectrum

Five levels of delegation, from most directive to most autonomous:

1. **Tell**: Manager decides and informs the team
2. **Sell**: Manager decides but explains the reasoning
3. **Consult**: Manager asks for input before deciding
4. **Agree**: Decision made jointly
5. **Delegate**: Team member decides within boundaries

### Common Delegation Fears

Managers often resist delegation because:
- "It's faster if I do it myself" — true short-term, false long-term
- Fear of mistakes — mitigated by defining clear boundaries and checkpoints
- Perfectionism — the 80% solution delivered by the team is better than the 100% solution bottlenecked by one person

### Building Empowerment

Empowerment is not a one-time event but a gradual process:
1. Start with low-risk tasks to build confidence
2. Increase scope as trust develops
3. Celebrate initiative, even when outcomes aren't perfect
4. Make failure a learning opportunity, not a punishment
