# Conflict Resolution Strategies

## Overall Summary

This lesson examines different approaches to resolving team conflicts, from avoidance to collaboration. It introduces the Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument as a diagnostic tool, provides scripts for difficult conversations, and explains when to escalate versus when to mediate directly.

## Key Points

- Conflict is natural and can be productive when managed well
- The Thomas-Kilmann model identifies five conflict styles: competing, accommodating, avoiding, compromising, collaborating
- Collaborating produces the best long-term outcomes but requires the most time and emotional energy
- Managers should separate the person from the problem — focus on interests, not positions
- Escalation to HR or senior leadership is appropriate when: safety is at risk, patterns repeat after intervention, or legal implications exist

## Detailed Notes

### The Five Conflict Styles

| Style | When Appropriate | Risk |
|-------|-----------------|------|
| Competing | Emergency decisions, safety issues | Damages relationships |
| Accommodating | Issue matters more to the other person | Can become a pattern of self-sacrifice |
| Avoiding | Trivial issues, need cooling-off time | Problems fester |
| Compromising | Time pressure, equally valid positions | Nobody fully satisfied |
| Collaborating | Important issues, relationship matters | Time-intensive |

### Scripts for Difficult Conversations

**Opening**: "I've noticed [specific behavior]. I'd like to understand your perspective."

**Redirecting**: "I hear that you're frustrated about X. Can we focus on what we can change going forward?"

**Closing**: "Here's what we've agreed to. Let's check in next week to see how it's going."

### Mediation Framework

When mediating between two team members:

1. Meet each person individually first — understand each perspective
2. Find common ground — what do both parties actually want?
3. Bring them together with ground rules (no interrupting, focus on behavior not character)
4. Guide toward a shared agreement
5. Follow up within one week
